- [cost-aware-bo-1] gap (open) — The 'Why vanilla BO fails' section grounds the EIpu critique solely in lee2020's empirical evidence (the KNN histogram, 9-of-20 problems). The stronger theoretical counterpart is omitted: astudillo2021 (cited and discussed in xie2024 line 60 and line 147) proves EIpu can perform 'arbitrarily worse than the optimal policy' in an approximation-ratio sense, due to oversampling low-value low-cost points. This is the rigorous statement of exactly the bias the note describes informally, and the relevant paper (astudillo2021) is already a source of the DP-branch notes this hub links to. Add one sentence to the EIpu failure-mode section noting the theoretical result (EIpu arbitrarily worse than optimal in an approximation-ratio sense, astudillo2021/xie2024), complementing lee2020's empirical critique; optionally cross-link to [[nonmyopic-cost-constrained-bo]]/[[multistep-budgeted-bo]] where astudillo2021 is developed.
- [cost-aware-bo-2] nit (open) — Line 19 attributes the two-formulation taxonomy ('the field frames best-f-per-cost in two distinct ways') to '(lee2020, xie2025)'. lee2020 only treats the budget-constrained formulation (minimize objective within a cost budget); it does not present the cost-per-sample / cost-adjusted-regret alternative. The budget-constrained-vs-cost-per-sample dichotomy is xie2024/xie2025's framing. The note already correctly attributes each individual formulation later (budget-constrained -> lee2020 at line 21; cost-per-sample -> xie2025 at line 31), so only the meta-claim's joint citation over-credits lee2020. Change the line-19 citation for the 'two distinct ways' meta-claim from '(lee2020, xie2025)' to '(xie2025)' (or 'xie2024, xie2025'), leaving the per-formulation citations as they are.
- [cost-aware-bo-3] nit (open) — The note states EIpu 'was introduced in snoek2012 as a practical heuristic (EI per second)' and presents it as EI/c. snoek2012's actual construction (draft.tex sec 'Modeling Costs', lines 401-425) is EI times the expected inverse duration, E[1/c]*EI, computed under a GP on ln c — not the point-estimate ratio EI/c. The clean EI/c form is what shahriari2016 (Sec VI-B) and lee2020 wrote when citing snoek2012. The conventional attribution is fine, but the note's crosswalk (line 132) flatly says snoek2012 'introduces EIpu as EI per second ... notation matches the canonical convention here already', which glosses over the E[1/c] vs 1/c distinction. In the [[ei-per-unit-cost]] child note (the natural home for the full derivation) note that snoek2012 actually used EI*E[1/c] and the EI/c point-estimate form is the downstream simplification; no change strictly required in this hub note, optionally soften the crosswalk wording.
