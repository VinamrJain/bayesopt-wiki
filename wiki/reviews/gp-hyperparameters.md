- [gp-hyperparameters-2] gap (open) — The 'early-iteration pitfall' section (lines 104-118) and its Remark over-attribute a specific mechanism to the sources. Neither snoek2012 nor frazier2018 states the 'n ≲ d' condition, nor that the marginal likelihood is 'flat or multi-modal in the length-scale directions', nor the EI/UCB-overconfidence-causing-the-loop-to-lock-onto-a-suboptimal-region chain. Snoek only argues empirically (Branin-Hoo, §4.1) that integrating over hyperparameters beats the point estimate; he does not qualify this as a small-n effect. Frazier §3.2 (line 223) supports only the weak general claim that the MLE 'sometimes estimates unreasonable hyperparameter values... vary too quickly or too slowly.' The mechanism is plausible and pedagogically useful but is not derivation-grade from these two sources. Either soften to claims the sources support (Frazier: MLE can be unreasonable at small data; Snoek: MCMC empirically beats Opt on Branin-Hoo, without the 'small n' qualifier), or cite a source that actually establishes the flat/multimodal-likelihood mechanism (e.g. Rasmussen & Williams Ch.5), and remove the unsupported 'at small n' framing from the Remark.
- [gp-hyperparameters-3] nit (open) — Body line 149 attributes the slice sampler to '(snoek2012; Neal, 2003)', but snoek2012 actually cites Murray & Adams (2010) for the slice sampler (draft.tex line 350), as the note's own crosswalk row (line 223) correctly records. Neal (2003) is the original slice-sampling paper and is the citation frazier2018 uses (line 235), so the attribution is not wrong, but the body and crosswalk are internally inconsistent about what snoek2012 cites. Make body and crosswalk consistent: either credit Neal (2003) as the slice-sampling origin while noting snoek2012 routes through Murray & Adams (2010), or just cite Neal (2003) for the method and snoek2012 for its use here.
- [gp-hyperparameters-4] gap (open) — The note says the same MCMC integration applies to 'any acquisition function' (lines 201-203) but the cross-link list omits the two acquisitions whose value is itself a posterior functional that the integration interacts with non-trivially: [[knowledge-gradient]] (whose μ*_n is a global posterior-mean max, frazier2018 §3.3) and [[thompson-sampling-bo]] / entropy-style acquisitions that already sample the posterior. A one-line note on whether integrated KG / TS is as clean as integrated EI would complete the connection. Add a brief cross-link or caveat noting that for KG/entropy-search/Thompson acquisitions the integrated form is well-defined but the per-sample object is more expensive than an EI evaluation; link [[knowledge-gradient]] and [[thompson-sampling-bo]].
