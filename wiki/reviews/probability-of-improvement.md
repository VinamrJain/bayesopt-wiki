- [probability-of-improvement-1] blocker (accepted-fixed) — The pathology box (lines 67-70) claims a point with Δ_n=0.1, σ_n=100 "scores the same" as one with Δ_n=0.1, σ_n=0.11. This is false. PI=Φ(Δ_n/σ_n) depends on σ_n through the ratio: Φ(0.1/100)=0.500 vs Φ(0.1/0.11)=0.818. PI does NOT ignore σ — for fixed Δ>0 it strictly DECREASES in σ (the high-uncertainty point gets the LOWER score), which is the actual mechanism of PI's over-exploitation. The note inverts the truth: it says uncertainty 'earns no direct reward,' but in fact uncertainty is actively penalized at fixed Δ>0 (the opposite of EI, whose σφ term rewards it). The central pedagogic illustration of PI's defining flaw is incorrect. Replace the example. Correct framing: at fixed Δ_n>0, raising σ_n LOWERS PI (drives Φ(Δ/σ) toward 0.5), so PI systematically prefers tight bets just over the incumbent and shuns high-uncertainty regions — that is the over-exploitation mechanism. Contrast: EI(σ=100)≈39.9 vs EI(σ=0.11)≈0.11 at the same Δ=0.1, i.e. EI rewards the very uncertainty PI penalizes. Keep the 'no σφ bonus' point but state the σ-dependence direction correctly.
