- [max-value-entropy-search-1] blocker (accepted-fixed) — Gumbel-section sign error. The note states the mean-field independence assumption 'over-estimates Pr[f*<z] and therefore produces samples that are stochastically upper bounds.' These two clauses are mutually inconsistent: a larger CDF Pr[f*<z] means stochastically SMALLER samples (lower bounds). The source is the other way round: the independent product ∏Φ(γ_z(x)) is an UNDER-estimate of the true (positively-correlated) max CDF (each factor <1, product smaller), which by Slepian's lemma yields stochastically larger samples = an upper bound on f* (method.tex L80-82, ap_gumbel.tex L14: 'gives us a distribution on the upperbound', 'tends to over-estimate the maximum'). The note conflated 'over-estimates the maximum' with 'over-estimates Pr[f*<z]'. The downstream conclusion (samples are upper bounds, vanishing regret) is correct; only the stated CDF direction is wrong. Change to: independence UNDER-estimates Pr[f*<z] (smaller CDF, since ∏Φ has many sub-unit factors), so the sampled max-value stochastically dominates the true f* — an upper bound; Slepian's lemma (k(x,x')≥0) makes this provable, and upper bounds still give vanishing regret. [fixed 2026-06-06]
- [max-value-entropy-search-2] gap (open) — Uncited specific complexity claim. The Remark (L131) asserts ES 'requires an N-point grid and an EP pass of cost O(N^4)'. - [max-value-entropy-search-3] nit (open) — Lemma/theorem numbering does not match the source. The note cites 'Lemma 1' (L121 for monotone g; L200 for the EST/UCB/PI equivalence) and 'Theorem 1' (L191) for the regret bound. The paper has no 'Lemma 1' or 'Theorem 1' — it uses section-prefixed numbering: the equivalence/monotonicity is Lemma 3.1 (lem:equivalence, method.tex), the regret is Theorem 3.x (thm:regret, regret.tex). Additionally, the limit 'g(u)→0⁺ as u→∞' is attributed to the lemma but the lemma states only monotonic decrease, not the limit (the limit is true, I re-derived it, just not in the cited lemma). Replace 'Lemma 1' → 'Lemma 3.1', 'Theorem 1' → 'Theorem 3.x' (or just 'the equivalence lemma' / 'the regret theorem'); drop the implication that the u→∞ limit is part of the lemma (state it as an elementary tail fact).
- [max-value-entropy-search-4] nit (open) — Noisy-case truncation glossed. The note frames conditioning on f*=y_* as 'f(x)≤y_*, so y is truncated to (−∞,y_*]'. Strictly this bounds f(x), not the noisy observation y=f(x)+ε. The source itself applies the truncation directly to y even in the noisy case (method.tex L33: 'the distribution of y needs to satisfy y<y_*'), which is itself an approximation. The note's 'noisy case adds λ to the variance' inherits this without flagging that truncating y (rather than f) is an approximation when noise is present. Add a half-sentence: in the noisy case truncating the predictive of y at y_* (rather than truncating f and re-convolving with noise) is the approximation the paper actually makes.
