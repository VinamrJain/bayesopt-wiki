- [bo-as-dynamic-program-1] nit (open) — The boxed terminal-utility Bellman recursion (V^N(s)=u(s); V^n(s)=max_x E[V^{n+1}(S^{n+1})|...]) is cited as '(lam2016, eqs. 9-10; frazier2009kg, sec 2.2, eq. 9)'. But lam2016 eqs. 9-10 are the STAGE-REWARD DP: J_k=max_u E[r_k + J_{k+1}(F_k)], with the per-stage reward r_k inside the expectation and terminal J_{N+1}=r_{N+1}. The note's boxed form (no stage reward, terminal = utility) is exactly frazier2009kg eq. 9 / astudillo2021 eq. 9, NOT lam2016 eqs. 9-10. lam2016's eqs. 9-10 are correctly the source of the telescoped form cited later. Attaching lam2016 eqs. 9-10 to the terminal-utility box is a citation mismatch. Drop 'lam2016, eqs. 9-10' from the boxed terminal-utility recursion citation (keep frazier2009kg eq. 9, optionally add astudillo2021 eq. 9). Cite lam2016 eqs. 9-10 only where the stage-reward/telescoped form is introduced.

- [bo-as-dynamic-program-6] nit (open) — The sufficient-statistic argument says the predictive law of every future observation depends on D_n 'only through (mu_n, sigma_n^2)'. For a GP the JOINT predictive over multiple future points depends on the full posterior mean and covariance FUNCTIONS, not just the marginal mean/variance at a point; the per-point marginal pair is sufficient only for a single one-step prediction. The note correctly calls the state infinite-dimensional two sentences later, so the (mu_n, sigma_n^2) phrasing is a local imprecision. Phrase the sufficient statistic as 'the full posterior (mean and covariance functions)' rather than the marginal pair (mu_n, sigma_n^2), keeping consistency with the infinite-dimensional-state remark that follows.
