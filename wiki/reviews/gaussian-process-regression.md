- [gaussian-process-regression-1] gap (open) — The crosswalk row for posterior variance asserts jones98's kriging MSE s^2(x) (their eq 9) is the 'same object' as the note's sigma_n^2(x). It is not exactly: jones98 eq (9) is s^2 = sigma^2[1 - r'R^{-1}r + (1 - 1'R^{-1}r)^2 / (1'R^{-1}1)]. The trailing term (1 - 1'R^{-1}r)^2/(1'R^{-1}1) is the extra variance from estimating the unknown constant mean mu by GLS (ordinary/universal kriging = empirical Bayes), and is absent from the simple-conditioning sigma_n^2 the note derives (which assumes a KNOWN prior mean mu_0). They coincide only in simple kriging (mu known). Likewise the predictor uses hat-mu (GLS estimate), not a fixed mu_0. Calling them 'same object' overstates the match and hides a real distinction (known-mean simple kriging vs. estimated-mean ordinary kriging). Soften the crosswalk: note that jones98 is ordinary/empirical-Bayes kriging with the mean estimated by MLE/GLS, so its variance carries an additional mean-uncertainty term (1-1'R^{-1}r)^2/(1'R^{-1}1) beyond the conditioning formula; the note's sigma_n^2 is the simple-kriging / known-mean case. Optionally add a one-line 'Notation/scope' caveat. (The mean-estimation tie-in belongs with [[gp-hyperparameters]].)
