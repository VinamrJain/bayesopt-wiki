- [thompson-sampling-bo-3] gap (open) — The TS/UCB 'equivalent in distribution' claim overstates the relationship and is not supported by shahriari2016. UCB maximizes mu_n(x)+beta*sigma_n(x), a deterministic functional applying one shared quantile beta at every x. TS maximizes a single draw of the jointly-correlated Gaussian field mu_n(x)+Z(x)*sigma_n(x). Choosing beta to match a standard-normal quantile equates only the per-point marginal at a fixed x; the argmax-over-domain selection distributions do not coincide, because TS's draw is one correlated surface, not an independent per-point quantile. The note already hedges with 'in practice they produce different sample paths,' which sits in tension with the preceding 'equivalent in distribution.'. Soften to something like: 'At any single x the TS draw g(x) and the UCB score share the same marginal when beta matches the realized standard-normal quantile; but UCB applies one fixed quantile uniformly across the domain whereas TS draws a correlated random surface, so their selection distributions differ.' Avoid the unqualified 'equivalent in distribution.'
