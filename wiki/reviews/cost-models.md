This contradicts a cited source. snoek2012 (draft.tex lines 420-422) explicitly says the objective/cost coupling 'may be usefully captured using GP variants of multi-task learning,' citing teh-etal-2005 and bonilla-etal-2008; lee2020's related work (main2020.tex line 144) further cites swersky2013's cost-aware multi-task entropy search. A standard workaround direction (multi-task / multi-output GPs) IS named in the cited sources. Replace the 'no workaround exists' sentence with: the sources note this coupling can be modeled jointly via multi-task / multi-output GPs (snoek2012 cites multi-task GP learning; cf. swersky2013 multi-task cost-aware BO), but the standard black-box default keeps the two GPs independent for tractability. Frame the independence as a tractability default with a known (if rarely used) joint-modeling alternative, not as an open problem with no answer. [fixed 2026-06-06]
