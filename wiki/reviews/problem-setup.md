- [problem-setup-2] blocker (open) — Crosswalk row (line 148) states jones98's EGO returns the 'minimum of DACE surface after convergence'. This is wrong: EGO iterates EI-maximization under a stopping rule (stop when E[I] < 1% of current best f_min, jones98 §4.1 lines 352-353) and reports the best *observed* point. jones98 explicitly argues *against* returning the DACE-surface minimum (lines 286, 296: 'The problem with simply finding the minimum of the DACE surface is that this procedure does not acknowledge our uncertainty'). The cell conflates the strawman EGO rejects with EGO's actual reported solution. Change the jones98 'Return' cell to 'best observed point at the 1%-EI stopping rule (f_min incumbent)'. EGO has no posterior-mean-reporting branch; it returns the incumbent, matching frazier's best-observed branch — the honest crosswalk is incumbent<->f_min, not DACE-minimum.
- [problem-setup-3] gap (open) — The entire 'What "solving" means: performance criteria' section (simple regret r_n = f* - f*_n, cumulative regret, r_N <= R_N/N) is sourced from neither cited source: frazier2018 never uses the word 'regret' (grep: zero hits) and jones98 frames success via a relative-error / EI stopping rule, not regret. - [problem-setup-4] nit (open) — Lines 109-110: best-observed arg max and best-posterior-mean arg max 'coincide in the noise-free case asymptotically'. Even noise-free, max_x mu_n(x) over the domain can exceed every observed value (the surrogate may predict a higher mean at an unevaluated point), so the two reporting rules need not coincide; coincidence holds only asymptotically under consistency/denseness. The qualifier 'asymptotically' rescues it but the sentence reads as if noise-freeness alone forces agreement. Frazier makes no such claim — this is the note's own synthesis. Tighten to: 'in the noise-free limit, under a consistent surrogate, the best posterior mean concentrates on observed optima; for finite n the two can differ (the surrogate may predict a higher mean at an unevaluated point).'
