- [gp-ucb-2] gap (open) — The note states the regret bounds are 'stated here, not proved' and defers the entire mechanism (confidence-ellipsoid log-volume growth related to gamma_T, union-bound bookkeeping) to a note that does not yet exist. srinivas2010 supports one extra load-bearing idea the note could surface in a single line: WHY gamma_T appears at all — the per-round information gain of the played point is 1/2 log(1+sigma^-2 sigma_{t-1}^2(x_t)), so summing instantaneous regrets (each bounded by ~beta_t^{1/2} sigma_{t-1}) telescopes into sqrt(T) times the total information gain. The note asserts the 'information gain <-> regret bridge' as a slogan but never names the sigma_{t-1}(x_t)^2 / information-per-sample link that is the actual hinge. Add one sentence near the regret section giving the hinge: the regret of the played point is controlled by its posterior std sigma_{t-1}(x_t), whose squared sum is exactly what gamma_T bounds (per-step information gain 1/2 log(1+sigma^-2 sigma_{t-1}^2)), so sum r_t <= sqrt(T sum sigma^2) <= sqrt(T gamma_T) up to the beta factor. This makes the 'bridge' a derivation pointer rather than a slogan, even with the full proof deferred.
- [gp-ucb-3] nit (open) — The Theorem 2 description says the finite-D log|D| 'is replaced by a discretization argument and beta_t picks up an additive 2d log(t^2 dbr sqrt(log(4da/delta))) term'. srinivas2010 Thm 2 actually has TWO additive pieces: 2 log(t^2 2pi^2/(3 delta)) PLUS the 2d log(...) term. The note silently drops the first piece. This is harmless as prose (the dropped piece is the same log t + const family already described for Thm 1), and the d-dependent term it keeps is the pedagogically relevant one, but a reader comparing to the paper will find the schedule incompletely transcribed. Either write the full Thm 2 schedule beta_t = 2 log(t^2 2pi^2/(3 delta)) + 2d log(t^2 dbr sqrt(log(4da/delta))), or change the prose from 'picks up an additive [term]' to 'picks up a d-dependent term 2d log(...) (alongside the same log t + const piece)' so it is clearly a summary, not a verbatim schedule.
- [gp-ucb-4] nit (open) — The 'upper quantile' claim is asserted but its quantitative meaning is left implicit. srinivas2010 ties beta_t^{1/2} to a Gaussian tail: for the single-point statement, P(f(x) > mu + beta^{1/2} sigma) = 1 - Phi(beta^{1/2}), so the union bound over |D| points and t^2 rounds with total budget delta forces beta_t = 2 log(|D| t^2 pi^2 / 6 delta) precisely so that the per-statement failure |D| t^2 (pi^2/6)^-1-weighted tail sums to delta. The note describes this verbally ('union bound forces beta_n to grow') but never connects 'upper quantile' to the standard-normal tail e^{-beta/2}, which is the one line that makes the 2 log(...) form non-magical. Add a half-sentence: since f(x)|D_n is Gaussian, P(f(x) > mu_n + beta^{1/2} sigma_n) <= e^{-beta/2}; setting this to delta/(|D| t^2 . 6/pi^2) per (point, round) and union-bounding gives exactly beta_t = 2 log(|D| t^2 pi^2 / 6 delta). This grounds both the 'upper quantile' phrase and the schedule's constants in one derivation step.
